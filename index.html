About

Hi! My name is Teodor. I'm a friendly person, open to new opportunities and willing to try on new technologies and ideas related to my field of study, but not only. I am studying Computer Science and Engineering at the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca, one of the most prestigious in the field in my country.

I have been fond of IT since early childhood, having my first computer at the age of 5. But only during high school I took my first steps into informatics. I had the opportunity to take part of an amazing group of informatics enthusiasts from my area while participating to informatics (algorithmic) and mathematics contests where I have obtained various awards.

I have also participated in an Erasmus Mobility Programme, in Ireland. This helped me get a broader view about how the field I'm studying is taught in other countries, what is the presence and standards of the IT industry in Ireland, and much about foreign culture in general.

I keep participating now and then at algorithmic contests, although this is no longer my main purpose. I am looking for opportunities which can enhance my career and help me gain relevant knowledge and experience for the future.

Multiplayer Cards Game

Project developed with a university colleague in Java. Consists of a multiplayer game of cards, a variant of Schnappsen, played by 4 players. The game features user registration, lobby with chat, possibility to create or join tables to play at. Developed for a university project, using Java SE, socket communications for server. Client was developed using JavaFX. Not yet deployed online.

  • Language/technologies: Java, client-server (sockets), JavaFX
  • Date: December 2014
  • URL: GitHub

Underwater Scene Game

Project consists of a 3D underwater world with fishes and user interaction. The user can swim in any direction using mouse and keyboard. Project was developed using C++ and OpenGL. Features simple fish animation (movement in random directions), caustics animation, projected and volumetric shadows, simple collision detection etc. Developed for a university graphics course.

  • Language/technologies: C++, OpenGL, OOP
  • Date: January 2015
  • URL: GitHub
